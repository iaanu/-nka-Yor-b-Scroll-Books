Philosophy of the Yorùbá Numerical Index

Numbers were never meant to be cold.

For centuries, the language of mathematics has spoken in monochrome. We forgot that counting began as song — that every culture once braided number and meaning, sky and soil, into a single living grammar.

Ònkà Yorùbá is the rediscovery of that grammar: a complete, base‑10 numeric system encoded in the Yorùbá tongue, scaling fluently from one to numbers larger than the atoms in a galaxy. Each name carries its own arithmetic — a root vowel, a tonal multiplier, a place‑value spine. Read aloud, the system computes.

🎵 And it sings. At the heart of every name beats one tiny chant of seven middles — èẹ́ · òẹ́ · òọ́ · èé · ẹ̀ẹ̀ · ẹ́ẹ́ · éẹ̀ … rǔn! 🥁 Sing it under Á, then change the first letter to É, Ẹ́, Í, Ó, Ọ́, Ú — and on through B, D, F … Z. Seven middles, one alphabet, 1,127 magnitudes reaching to 10135. A nursery rhyme that scales to the stars.
